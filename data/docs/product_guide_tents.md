# Product Guide: Tents

## Overview

Acme Outfitters' tent line spans ultralight backpacking shelters to large family car-camping
tents. All tents in our lineup use a double-wall construction (except bivy sacks) to manage
condensation and provide weather protection.

## Materials & Technologies

### Fabrics

| Rating | What it means |
|---|---|
| 1500mm HH | Light rain, suitable for 3-season camping |
| 2000mm HH | Moderate rain, standard 3-season |
| 3000mm HH | Heavy rain, suitable for extended trips |
| 5000mm HH+ | Alpine and expedition use |

All our fabrics are treated with a Durable Water Repellent (DWR) finish. DWR degrades over
time and can be refreshed with a DWR spray or low-heat dryer treatment.

### Poles

- **DAC Featherlight:** High-strength aluminum alloy, lightest without sacrificing durability.
  Used in our 3-season backpacking line.
- **DAC Featherlight NFL (No-Fault Lock):** Expedition-grade hubs with fail-safe locking.
  Used in 4-season and alpine tents.
- **Carbon fiber:** Used in the Ultralight Solo tent only; stiffer and lighter than aluminum
  but less impact-resistant.
- **Fiberglass:** Used only in entry-level and family camping models (FamDome 8).

## Care & Maintenance

### Setting Up

- Always use a footprint (sold separately) to protect the tent floor from abrasion and puncture.
- Clear the tent site of sharp rocks and sticks before pitching.
- In strong wind, stake the tent before raising the poles.

### Drying

**Always dry your tent completely before packing for storage.** Packing a wet tent promotes
mildew growth that will permanently damage fabric and stitching.

- Set up the tent with the rain fly off to dry interior condensation.
- Hang the fly and footprint separately.
- Do not dry in direct sunlight for extended periods — UV degrades coatings.
- Do not use a dryer.

### Cleaning

- Wipe down with a damp cloth and mild soap (no bleach or harsh detergents).
- Rinse the floor bathtub with water to remove dirt and grit.
- Re-apply DWR spray annually or when water beads have stopped forming on the fly.

### Storage

- Store loosely in a breathable storage sack (not the stuff sack) in a cool, dry location.
- Never store in a hot car or attic where heat can degrade coatings and elastics.

### Seam Sealing

All tents are factory seam-sealed. For expedition use or after several seasons, re-seam-sealing
is recommended. Use McNett Seam Grip or a silnylon-compatible sealant for silnylon models.
