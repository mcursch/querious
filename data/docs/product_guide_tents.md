# Product Guide: Tents & Shelters — Acme Outfitters

## Overview

Acme Outfitters' tent line is organized into four series designed to cover the full range of camping and backpacking conditions. Each series uses a consistent naming convention: **ACM-TENT-XXX** SKUs. This guide covers materials, construction methods, intended use cases, setup tips, and care instructions for all current tent models.

All Acme tents carry a **2-year warranty** against manufacturing defects. See the Warranty Policy for full details and the claim process.

## Tent Series

### TrailLite Series — Ultralight Backpacking

The TrailLite series is designed for gram-conscious backpackers who prioritize pack weight above all else. These tents use **20-denier silnylon** for the fly and **15-denier ripstop nylon** for the inner canopy, saving weight at a moderate reduction in long-term durability versus heavier fabrics.

**Key features:**
- Freestanding or semi-freestanding designs (model-dependent)
- DAC Featherlight NSL aluminum poles — industry-standard reliability at minimal weight
- Fully seam-taped fly with a **1,500 mm hydrostatic head rating** — suitable for moderate rain; not rated for extended heavy downpours
- Minimum weight (bare poles, fly, inner): **1 lb 14 oz (TrailLite 1P)** to **2 lb 12 oz (TrailLite 2P)**
- Packed size: fits inside most 65L packs' top lid pocket

**Intended use:** 3-season backpacking, below-treeline camping, UL thru-hiking

**Not recommended for:** Above-treeline exposed camping in high wind, snow loads, or sustained rain over 24 hours

### AlpineGuard Series — 4-Season Mountain Tents

The AlpineGuard series is built for mountaineering, ski touring, and above-treeline expeditions where wind and snow are expected. Construction uses **40-denier ripstop nylon** throughout and **7000-series aluminum** crossing poles for dome rigidity under load.

**Key features:**
- Full geodesic or modified dome designs — pole crossings provide structural resistance to wind and snow load
- Fly hydrostatic head rating: **3,000 mm** — suitable for heavy rain, wet snow, and condensation-prone cold-weather camping
- Factory-seam-taped (Acme applies seam tape in our factory, not just at point-of-sale)
- Vestibule area: 10–14 sq ft (varies by model) for gear storage
- Rated for **wind gusts up to 60 mph** when properly staked and guyed with included guidelines
- Interior peak height: 42–48 inches (1P and 2P models)

**Intended use:** Alpine climbing, ski mountaineering, extreme weather camping, polar expeditions

**Not recommended for:** Car camping (overkill weight and price); summer festival camping

### BaseCamp Series — Car Camping & Base Camps

The BaseCamp series prioritizes livability, headroom, and ease of setup for groups camping from a vehicle or a fixed base camp. These tents use **75-denier polyester** for the fly and walls — heavier and more UV-resistant than nylon, suitable for extended stays in the sun.

**Key features:**
- Hub-and-sleeve pole system for fast setup (most models set up in under 10 minutes)
- Fly hydrostatic head: **2,000 mm**
- Interior peak height: 68–76 inches (6P model) — full standing room
- Large mesh panels for ventilation in warm weather
- Multiple doors (2 on most 4P+ models) and interior pockets
- Available in 2P, 4P, and 6P configurations

**Intended use:** Car camping, family camping, 3-season base camps, music festivals

### UltraShield Series — Expedition & Extended Base Camps

The UltraShield series represents Acme's flagship expedition tents, used by guided expedition teams and professional mountaineers. They are built to be repaired in the field, with modular pole segments, replaceable guy-line anchors, and available repair kits.

**Key features:**
- **60-denier Dyneema®-reinforced ripstop** fly — exceptional tear resistance at moderate weight
- Fly hydrostatic head: **5,000 mm**
- Color-coded pole clips and clips-over-hub system for reliable setup in poor visibility
- Snow valance sewn into the perimeter — can be weighted with snow for polar wind resistance
- Internal cord-lock vents allow ventilation adjustment without opening the door
- Rated to **sustained 70-mph wind** when fully guyed (8 guy points)

**Intended use:** Polar expeditions, Himalayan base camps, multi-week alpine climbing camps

## Setup Best Practices

1. **Always stake out the corners first** before raising the poles. A pre-staked footprint keeps the tent floor from shifting during assembly and reduces stress on pole sleeves.
2. **Pitch the fly before the inner in wet conditions.** On AlpineGuard and UltraShield models, the fly can be deployed first with the inner clipped in from underneath, keeping the inner dry.
3. **Guy out in any wind above 20 mph.** Unstaked and un-guyed tents can be damaged or destroyed by wind even below the tent's rated maximum.
4. **Never use an open flame inside any tent** — including candle lanterns and certain chemical hand warmers. Nylon and polyester fabrics melt and burn rapidly.

## Care & Storage

- **Dry completely before storage.** Storing a damp tent leads to mildew and hydrolysis of polyurethane coatings. If you must pack wet, unpack and dry within 24 hours.
- **Store loosely, not compressed.** Long-term compression of the packed stuff sack degrades pole elasticity and fabric loft. Store in a large mesh bag or hung in a dry closet.
- **Clean with cold water and mild soap only.** Do not machine-wash tent flies or use detergents containing bleach, solvents, or fabric softeners.
- **Re-treat DWR as needed.** When water no longer beads on the fly surface, apply a wash-in or spray-on DWR treatment (Nikwax Tent & Gear SolarProof recommended).
- **Inspect pole ferrules** after each trip. Clean sand and grit from the ends to prevent sleeve wear.
