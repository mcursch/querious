# Product Guide — Tents and Shelters

*Acme Outfitters Tent Line, 2025 Edition*

## Overview

Acme Outfitters carries three tent families: the **TrailCrest** series for ultralight backpacking, the **BaseCamp** series for three-season car camping and base camping, and the **AlpineForce** series for four-season mountaineering. This guide describes the materials, specifications, setup, and care for each family, and provides guidance for choosing the right tent.

---

## TrailCrest Series — Ultralight Backpacking

The TrailCrest line is engineered for minimalists who count grams. All TrailCrest tents use a trekking-pole-supported or freestanding single-wall design depending on the model.

### TrailCrest 1 (Solo)

- **Trail weight:** 1 lb 3 oz (544 g)
- **Packed weight:** 1 lb 5 oz (595 g)
- **Floor area:** 20 sq ft
- **Peak height:** 38 in

- **Fabric — fly:** 20D silnylon, 1,500 mm hydrostatic head
- **Fabric — floor:** 30D silpoly DCF laminate, 3,000 mm hydrostatic head
- **Seams:** Factory-taped
- **Vestibule:** Single, 7 sq ft
- **Best for:** Solo thru-hikers, ultralight enthusiasts, summer through early fall

- **Packed weight:** 1 lb 15 oz (879 g)

- **Fabric — fly:** 20D silnylon, 1,500 mm hydrostatic head

## BaseCamp Series — Three-Season Car Camping

BaseCamp tents prioritize livability, ventilation, and ease of setup over ultralight weight. They are freestanding double-wall designs built for 3-season use (spring through fall in most US climates).

### BaseCamp 4 (Family)

- **Capacity:** 4 persons (comfortably sleeps 3 adults with gear)
- **Trail weight:** 9 lb 8 oz
- **Floor area:** 56 sq ft (inner) + 15 sq ft vestibule
- **Peak height:** 72 in — full stand-up height
- **Poles:** 4 × DAC Featherlite aluminum; color-coded for fast pitch
- **Fly fabric:** 68D polyester ripstop, 1,800 mm HH, PU + silicone coating
- **Inner body:** 40D nylon mesh upper (65% mesh / 35% solid nylon)
- **Floor fabric:** 68D polyester taffeta, 3,000 mm HH
- **Seams:** Factory-taped fly; taped floor seams
- **Setup time:** Approx. 8 minutes (two-person pitch)
- **Vestibule:** Single, 15 sq ft with vertical zipper for drive-through access to large items
- **Best for:** Family car camping, group trips, base camps with vehicle access

### BaseCamp 2 (Touring)

- **Capacity:** 2 persons
- **Trail weight:** 4 lb 12 oz
- **Floor area:** 32 sq ft + dual 9 sq ft vestibules
- **Peak height:** 48 in
- **Poles:** 2 × DAC Featherlite aluminum hubbed poles
- **Fabric:** Same spec as BaseCamp 4
- **Best for:** Motorcycle camping, canoe-tripping, bike touring where some weight savings matter but not at the expense of livability

## AlpineForce Series — Four-Season Mountaineering

AlpineForce tents are designed to handle sustained wind, snow loading, and sub-zero temperatures. They are geodesic freestanding designs with no mesh in the inner body.

### AlpineForce 2

- **Capacity:** 2 persons (3 in emergency)
- **Trail weight:** 7 lb 2 oz
- **Floor area:** 29 sq ft
- **Peak height:** 44 in
- **Poles:** 3 × 9 mm DAC Hybrid aluminum (reinforced at joints); color-coded; hub-connected
- **Fly fabric:** 40D silicone/PU nylon, 3,000 mm HH; reinforced at stake-out points
- **Inner body:** 40D nylon, fully solid (no mesh)
- **Floor fabric:** 70D nylon taffeta, 10,000 mm HH
- **Seams:** Triple-stitched and fully tape-sealed
- **Tested wind rating:** 60 mph continuous, 80 mph gusts (ASTM F2682 equivalent)
- **Snow load:** Up to 40 lbs total on fly
- **Vestibule:** Dual, 8 sq ft each; no-touch entry to keep snow out of the sleeping area
- **Best for:** Winter camping, alpine climbing, expeditions above treeline

### AlpineForce 3

- **Capacity:** 3 persons
- **Trail weight:** 9 lb 1 oz
- **Floor area:** 38 sq ft
- **Peak height:** 46 in
- **Poles:** 4 × 9 mm DAC Hybrid aluminum
- **All other specs:** Same as AlpineForce 2

## Materials Glossary

- **Silnylon:** Nylon ripstop coated on both sides with silicone. Lighter than PU-coated nylon; does not absorb water; ages well if stored dry.
- **Silpoly DCF:** Polyester ripstop with a laminated Dyneema Composite Fabric layer. Higher tear strength than silnylon at equivalent weight.
- **HH (Hydrostatic Head):** Resistance to water pressure in millimeters. 1,500 mm = adequate for most rain; 3,000 mm+ = suitable for driving rain and condensation pools; 10,000 mm = highly waterproof floor.
- **DAC Featherlite:** Aluminum alloy poles optimized for strength-to-weight in three-season conditions.
- **DAC Hybrid:** DAC's stiffer pole alloy for high-wind and snow-load applications.

## Care and Maintenance

### After Every Trip

1. Shake out debris from tent body and fly before packing.
2. Air-dry completely before storage — packing a wet tent even briefly can initiate mildew growth and degrade the waterproof coating.
3. Wipe pole segments with a dry cloth; inspect pole tips for cracks.

### Periodic Maintenance

- **Re-waterproofing:** The DWR (Durable Water Repellency) coating on the fly will eventually wear off, causing the fabric to "wet out" (absorb water rather than beading). Apply Acme Outfitters DWR Spray or a compatible product after washing, while the fly is still damp. Tumble on low heat for 10 minutes to activate.
- **Seam re-taping:** If seam tape begins to peel, apply seam sealer (silicone-based for silnylon, urethane-based for PU-coated fabrics) to the affected area and allow to cure fully before use.
- **Zipper care:** Apply a zipper lubricant (Zipper Rescue or equivalent) to all zippers twice per season. Do not force a zipper; if a slider is skipping teeth, realign the coils manually before applying force.

### Storage

Store tents loosely in a large breathable cotton or mesh bag — not in the factory compression stuff sack for long-term storage, which fatigues the coatings. Store in a cool, dark location away from UV light and away from petroleum products (fuel canisters, lubricants).
