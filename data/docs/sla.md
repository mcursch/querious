# Support Service Level Agreement (SLA)

*Effective January 1, 2025. Applies to all inbound support contacts.*

## Overview

Acme Outfitters' Support team is committed to resolving customer issues quickly and transparently. This document defines response-time commitments, escalation paths, and measurement methodology for all support channels. SLA performance is reviewed monthly by the VP of Customer Experience.

## Ticket Priority Levels

All support tickets are assigned a priority level at intake, either by the customer selecting it or by the triage agent based on the nature of the request.

### Urgent

Situations that prevent a customer from completing a time-sensitive activity with serious safety or business impact. Examples:
- Safety concern about a product in use (stove malfunction, harness defect reported in the field)
- Enterprise account experiencing a complete order-processing outage
- Payment failure on a time-sensitive bulk order for a scheduled event

### High

Significant issues with a clear business or trip impact that are not immediately life-threatening. Examples:
- Order shipped with wrong items less than 3 days before a trip
- Invoice dispute over $1,000
- Warranty claim for critical safety equipment (harness, helmet)

### Medium

Standard service requests with moderate impact. Examples:
- Return and refund request
- Order tracking inquiry where package shows delayed
- Standard warranty claim for non-safety gear

### Low

Informational requests and minor issues. Examples:
- Product availability or specification questions
- Address or account-detail updates
- General feedback or suggestions

## Response Time Commitments

"First response" means a substantive reply from a human agent (not an automated acknowledgement) that addresses the ticket content.

"Resolution" means the issue is fully resolved or the customer has been given a clear next step and timeline.

### Standard Tier Customers

| Priority | First Response | Target Resolution |
|----------|---------------|-------------------|
| Urgent | 4 business hours | 24 business hours |
| High | 1 business day | 3 business days |
| Medium | 2 business days | 7 business days |
| Low | 3 business days | 10 business days |

### Premium Tier Customers

Premium tier customers receive priority queue routing and faster initial response times.

| Priority | First Response | Target Resolution |
|----------|---------------|-------------------|
| Urgent | 2 business hours | 8 business hours |
| High | 4 business hours | 2 business days |
| Medium | 1 business day | 4 business days |
| Low | 2 business days | 7 business days |

### Enterprise Tier Customers

Enterprise customers receive the highest level of support, including dedicated agents and a direct escalation path to the VP of Customer Experience.

| Priority | First Response | Target Resolution |
|----------|---------------|-------------------|
| Urgent | 1 hour (business hours); 2 hours (after hours with on-call provision) | 4 business hours |
| High | 2 business hours | 1 business day |
| Medium | 4 business hours | 3 business days |
| Low | 1 business day | 5 business days |

## Escalation Process

### First Escalation (Tier 2 Agent)

If a ticket has not received a first response within 125% of the SLA time, it is automatically escalated to a Tier 2 Support agent and flagged for supervisor review.

### Second Escalation (Support Supervisor)

Tickets that are not resolved within 150% of the target resolution time are escalated to the Support Supervisor. The customer is notified by email with an updated timeline commitment.

### Third Escalation (VP of Customer Experience)

For Enterprise accounts, any ticket at Urgent or High priority that exceeds the target resolution time is escalated to the VP of Customer Experience, who will personally contact the enterprise account manager within one business day.

---

## SLA Exclusions

Response and resolution clocks are paused in the following situations:

- **Waiting for customer response** — if the Support agent has asked the customer for additional information and has not received a reply, the clock is paused until the customer responds.
- **Third-party carrier investigation** — if resolution requires a carrier claim or investigation outside Acme's control, the resolution clock is paused pending the carrier's response; Acme will provide status updates every 2 business days.
- **Force majeure** — natural disasters, widespread carrier outages, and other events beyond Acme's reasonable control.

---

## SLA Reporting and Credits

### Monthly SLA Report

Acme Outfitters publishes a monthly SLA performance report to Enterprise account managers summarizing first-response and resolution rates by priority level. Standard and Premium customers may request a summary of their own ticket history at any time.

### SLA Credits

Enterprise customers with a contracted SLA addendum are eligible for service credits when Acme fails to meet the committed response or resolution times. Credit amounts are defined in the individual account agreement. Credits are applied as account balance toward future invoices and do not exceed the monthly service fee or, for non-subscription accounts, 5% of the average monthly spend over the prior 3 months.
