# Support Service Level Agreement (SLA) — Acme Outfitters

_Last updated: January 15, 2025_
_Effective for all tickets opened on or after February 1, 2025_

---

## 1. Purpose

This document defines the support response-time commitments Acme Outfitters makes to customers
based on their account tier (**Standard**, **Premium**, or **Enterprise**) and the priority level
assigned to each support ticket. These commitments apply to the Acme Outfitters Customer Support
team and are measured during our published support hours unless otherwise noted.

support window).

## 4. Ticket Priority Definitions

| Priority | Description | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Urgent** | Issue prevents the customer from completing a critical business operation or poses a safety risk. | Order fulfillment completely blocked; safety-recall inquiry; confirmed payment fraud. |
| **High** | Major functionality impaired; significant customer impact; time-sensitive. | Invoice discrepancy over $500; order shipped to wrong address; warranty claim for a trip departing within 7 days. |
| **Medium** | Non-critical issue or question with moderate impact; workaround exists. | General return or exchange request; product availability question; account billing question. |
| **Low** | General inquiry, feedback, or cosmetic issue with no time sensitivity. | Product recommendation request; feedback on website; request for catalog. |

Priority is assigned by the customer at ticket creation and may be adjusted by the Support team if
the stated priority does not match the described situation.

## 5. Response-Time SLA Matrix

"First Response" is the time from ticket submission to the first substantive reply from a support
agent (automated acknowledgment emails do not count). "Resolution Target" is the target time to
fully resolve or escalate the ticket.

### 5.1 Standard Tier

| Priority | First Response | Resolution Target |
|---|---|---|
| Urgent | 8 business hours | 3 business days |
| High | 1 business day | 5 business days |
| Medium | 3 business days | 10 business days |
| Low | 5 business days | 15 business days |

### 5.2 Premium Tier

| Priority | First Response | Resolution Target |
|---|---|---|
| Urgent | 4 business hours | 1 business day |
| High | 8 business hours | 3 business days |
| Medium | 1 business day | 5 business days |
| Low | 2 business days | 10 business days |

### 5.3 Enterprise Tier

| Priority | First Response | Resolution Target |
|---|---|---|
| Urgent | **1 calendar hour** (24 × 7) | **4 calendar hours** |
| High | **2 business hours** | 1 business day |
| Medium | 4 business hours | 3 business days |
| Low | 1 business day | 5 business days |

> **Note:** Enterprise Urgent SLAs are measured in **calendar hours around the clock**, not
> business hours. All other Enterprise SLAs are measured in business hours within the Enterprise
> support window (Mon–Sun 7 AM–10 PM MT).

## 6. SLA Credits

If Acme Outfitters fails to meet a committed First Response time, affected customers are eligible
for the following credits applied to their next invoice or as store credit:

| Tier | SLA Miss Credit |
|---|---|
| Standard | $10.00 store credit per missed First Response |
| Premium | $25.00 store credit per missed First Response |
| Enterprise | 5 % credit on the current month's service fees, per incident (up to $500 per month) |

Credits are issued automatically when our system detects a SLA breach. Customers may also request
a credit review by contacting **sla@acmeoutfitters.example** within **30 days** of the breach.
Credits are non-transferable and have no cash value.

---

## 7. Escalation Path

If a ticket is not resolved within the Resolution Target:

1. **Automatic escalation** — The ticket is flagged in our system and assigned to a senior support
   specialist.
2. **Manager review** — A Support Manager contacts the customer within 4 business hours of the
   automatic escalation.
3. **Director review (Enterprise only)** — If still unresolved after an additional 1 business day,
   the Director of Customer Experience is looped in and personally oversees resolution.

Customers may also manually request escalation at any time by replying to their ticket with the
word "ESCALATE" in the subject line, or by calling the Enterprise phone line (Enterprise tier
only).

---

## 8. Exclusions

The following situations are excluded from SLA commitments:

- Tickets submitted outside of support hours (clock starts at the beginning of the next support
  window, except Enterprise Urgent which is 24/7).
- Tickets requiring third-party carrier investigation (carrier trace timelines are outside Acme
  Outfitters' control; we commit only to initiating the trace within SLA).
- Force-majeure events (natural disasters, widespread telecom outages, government actions).
- Situations where the customer has not responded to a request for information for more than
  **3 business days** — the ticket is placed on "Pending Customer" status and the SLA clock is
  paused.

---

## 9. SLA Measurement and Reporting

- All SLA metrics are logged in our support platform (Zendesk) and auditable.
- **Enterprise customers** receive a monthly SLA performance report by the 5th of the following
  month, showing ticket volume, average first-response time, resolution rates, and any SLA breaches
  with credit details.
- **Premium customers** may request a quarterly summary by emailing **sla@acmeoutfitters.example**.

---

## 10. Tier Upgrade Path

Customers on Standard tier who experience repeated SLA misses (3 or more in a rolling 90-day
period) are eligible for a **complimentary 30-day trial of Premium support**. Customers may upgrade
their tier at any time; see the **Pricing Tiers** document for costs and benefits.

---

## 11. Policy Review

This SLA is reviewed annually and updated as needed. Customers are notified of material changes
at least **30 days** in advance via email to the account billing address. The current version is
always available at **acmeoutfitters.example/legal/sla**.
