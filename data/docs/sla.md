# Service Level Agreement (SLA) — Acme Outfitters Support

## Purpose

This document defines the support response-time commitments Acme Outfitters makes to its customers. Commitments vary by ticket priority level and the customer's tier (Standard, Premium, or Enterprise). All times listed are **business hours** unless otherwise noted. Business hours are Monday–Friday, 8:00 AM – 6:00 PM Mountain Time, excluding federal holidays.

Customers with urgent safety-related issues (e.g., a recalled or defective climbing product) may always call our emergency line at 1-800-555-ACME regardless of tier or business hours.

---

## Priority Definitions

Each support ticket is assigned a priority by the Support team at intake based on the impact and urgency of the issue:

| Priority | Definition | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Urgent** | Business operations halted or safety issue; immediate revenue or safety impact | Product safety recall, order system down for enterprise buyer, shipment with dangerous goods mislabeled |
| **High** | Significant customer impact; workaround available but painful | Order shipped to wrong address, invoice overdue blocking net-30 renewal, warranty item causing customer harm |
| **Medium** | Noticeable issue with a reasonable workaround | Return label not working, incorrect item in shipment discovered before use, billing discrepancy under $200 |
| **Low** | Minor or informational; no active disruption | General product questions, feedback, low-urgency account updates, address changes for future orders |

Priority assignment is reviewed by the Support lead at the start of each business day. Customers may request a priority upgrade by contacting their account manager; the Support lead has final authority on priority classification.

---

## Response-Time Commitments by Priority and Tier

"**First response**" means a human agent acknowledges the ticket and provides either a resolution or a clear next-step timeline. Auto-acknowledgment emails do not count toward first-response SLA.

"**Resolution target**" is the target time to fully resolve the issue. Complex issues (e.g., lost freight shipments, multi-item warranty claims) may take longer; the agent will communicate a revised timeline when the resolution target cannot be met.

### Standard Tier

| Priority | First Response | Resolution Target |
|---|---|---|
| **Urgent** | **4 business hours** | 2 business days |
| **High** | **8 business hours** | 4 business days |
| **Medium** | **24 business hours** | 7 business days |
| **Low** | **72 business hours** | 14 business days |

### Premium Tier

| **High** | **4 business hours** | 2 business days |
| **Medium** | **12 business hours** | 4 business days |

### Enterprise Tier

| Priority | First Response | Resolution Target |
|---|---|---|

| **High** | **2 business hours** | 1 business day |
| **Medium** | **8 business hours** | 3 business days |
| **Low** | **24 business hours** | 7 business days |

## After-Hours Coverage

Standard and Premium tier tickets submitted outside business hours are queued and addressed when business hours resume the next business day. The SLA clock for Standard and Premium customers starts at the beginning of the next business day for tickets submitted after 6:00 PM MT or on weekends/holidays.

Enterprise tier customers with a signed Enterprise Agreement that includes 24/7 SLA coverage have a separate addendum to this policy. The SLA clock for Enterprise Urgent tickets runs 24/7 regardless of when the ticket was submitted. High-priority Enterprise tickets submitted outside business hours begin their 2-business-hour SLA clock at 8:00 AM MT the next business day, unless the Enterprise Agreement specifies extended-hours High coverage.

---

## Escalation Process

If an SLA commitment is missed, customers should:

1. Reply to their existing ticket and type "ESCALATE" in the subject or first line of the message. This flags the ticket for the Support lead queue.
2. Contact their account manager directly (Premium and Enterprise only).
3. Email escalations@acmeoutfitters.example with the ticket number.

Escalated tickets are reviewed by the Support lead within 1 business hour during business hours. Repeated SLA misses against the same account are logged and reviewed in the quarterly business review for that account.

---

## Exclusions & Limitations

These SLA commitments do not apply to:

- Incidents caused by factors outside Acme Outfitters' control (carrier delays, natural disasters, third-party system outages)
- Tickets submitted with insufficient information (wrong order number, no contact details) — the SLA clock is paused until the customer provides required information
- Tickets subsequently found to be duplicate submissions

Acme Outfitters does not offer SLA credits or financial penalties for missed response times unless a specific SLA credit clause is included in a signed Enterprise Agreement.

---

## SLA Review & Updates

This SLA is reviewed annually by the VP of Customer Experience and the Head of Sales. Changes take effect 30 days after notice to customers. The most current version of this document is always available in the customer portal and the internal Querious knowledge base.
