# Product Guide: Stoves & Cook Systems — Acme Outfitters

## Overview

Acme Outfitters' stove line is organized by fuel type and use case, covering ultralight backpacking canister stoves, liquid-fuel expedition stoves, and integrated cook system sets. Stoves use the **ACM-STOVE-XXX** SKU prefix.

All Acme stoves carry a **1-year warranty** against manufacturing defects. See the Warranty Policy for details. This is a shorter warranty than our tents and packs due to the mechanical and heat-cycle complexity of combustion components.

> ⚠️ **Safety Notice:** Always read the full safety instructions included with your stove before first use. Improper use of camp stoves can result in fire, explosion, carbon monoxide poisoning, or severe burns. Never use any camp stove — canister or liquid-fuel — inside a tent, enclosed shelter, or vehicle.

## Fuel Types

### Isobutane-Propane Canister (Recommended for Most Users)

Isobutane-propane canisters (also called "canister fuel" or "iso-butane") are the most convenient option for backpacking and camping. Canisters are pre-pressurized, require no priming, and produce no spills.

**Best for:** 3-season backpacking, family camping, general use

**Limitations:**
- Performance drops at temperatures below 20°F (-7°C) as canister pressure decreases
- Canisters cannot be refilled (single-use; recycle at hazardous-waste facilities)
- Canister fuel is not available on airline carry-on or checked luggage (prohibited by IATA)
- Canister fuel is not available at all trailheads and international destinations

Acme canister stoves are compatible with the **Lindal valve standard** (EN 417), which means they work with most major canister brands (MSR, Jetboil, Primus, Snow Peak). We recommend Acme-brand canisters for best performance, but cross-brand compatibility is reliable.

### White Gas (Liquid Fuel)

White gas (also called naphtha or Coleman fuel) is a refined liquid fuel used in multi-fuel and dedicated white-gas stoves. It burns very hot, performs well in cold weather and at altitude, and can be purchased in bulk.

**Best for:** Winter camping, mountaineering, expeditions, international travel

**Limitations:**
- Stoves require priming (preheating the fuel line) before use
- More maintenance required than canister stoves (cleaning jets, replacing pump cups)
- Spills are a fire risk during filling

### Multi-Fuel

Multi-fuel stoves (Acme UltraFlame MF series) accept white gas, unleaded gasoline, diesel, and aviation fuel. These are designed for expedition and military use where fuel availability is unpredictable.

## Safety Notes

### Carbon Monoxide Warning

Camp stoves, including canister stoves, produce **carbon monoxide (CO)**, an odorless, colorless, potentially lethal gas. **Never operate any stove in a tent, vestibule, enclosed vehicle, or any space without active ventilation.** CO poisoning symptoms include headache, dizziness, nausea, and confusion — if you suspect CO exposure, move immediately to fresh air.

### Canister Disposal

Do not puncture or throw canisters into fire. Even "empty" canisters contain residual pressurized gas. To safely dispose of a canister, use a canister puncturing tool (sold separately) to fully depressurize before placing in standard recycling. Check local recycling guidelines, as some jurisdictions classify fuel canisters as hazardous waste requiring special disposal.

### White Gas Storage

Store white gas in approved metal fuel containers in a cool, ventilated location away from ignition sources. Do not store fuel inside a vehicle or tent overnight.

### Stove Maintenance

Clean fuel jets on liquid-fuel stoves after every 3–5 uses or when output becomes inconsistent. Replacement jet needles, pump cups, and O-rings are available as a service kit (SKU ACM-STOVE-KIT-001) for all UltraFlame models.

## Care Instructions

- Allow stoves to cool completely before packing.
- Remove canister from canister stoves before storage; storing with an attached canister can cause O-ring compression and slow leaks.
- Wipe the burner head with a dry cloth after each use to remove food residue.
- Store stoves in their included stuff sacks to protect the igniter and valve from impact damage.
