## Fuel Types

Understanding fuel types ensures safe and efficient stove operation in different conditions.

### Isobutane/Propane Blend Canisters (used by ACM-STOVE-001, ACM-STOVE-003)
- **Description:** Pre-pressurized canisters containing a mixture of ~80 % isobutane and ~20 %
  propane. Sold in 100 g, 230 g, and 450 g sizes.
- **Best conditions:** Spring, summer, fall; temperatures above −10 °C (14 °F).
- **Limitations:** Output drops significantly at temperatures below 0 °C as canister pressure
  falls; near-empty canisters underperform in cold weather.
- **Disposal:** Puncture and recycle per local hazardous waste regulations; do **not** throw
  full or partially full canisters in regular trash or incinerate.
- **Storage:** Away from heat sources and direct sunlight; never stored above 50 °C.

### White Gas / Coleman Fuel (used by ACM-STOVE-002)
- **Description:** Highly refined, low-sulfur liquid fuel. Burns cleanly with high heat output.
- **Best conditions:** Cold weather, high altitude; remains effective at −40 °C.
- **Availability:** Widely available in outdoor retailers globally.
- **Notes:** Requires priming (preheat fuel line) before full flame. Produces more soot than
  canister fuel; clean jet more frequently.

### Unleaded Gasoline (used by ACM-STOVE-002 — emergency use)
- **Description:** Standard automotive unleaded fuel.
- **Best conditions:** Expedition scenarios where white gas is unavailable.
- **Limitations:** Contains additives that foul the jet faster; clean jet after every 2–3 uses.
  Not recommended for regular use.

### Kerosene / Diesel (used by ACM-STOVE-002 — emergency use)
- **Description:** Denser hydrocarbons.
- **Best conditions:** Developing-world expeditions where only diesel is available.
- **Limitations:** Require longer priming; produce more soot; yellow/orange flame is normal.
  Not recommended for regular use.

### Solid Fuel Tablets / Esbit Hexamine (used by ACM-STOVE-004)
- **Description:** Pre-compressed hexamine fuel tablets. No liquid, no pressure.
- **Best conditions:** Emergency use, ultralight travel, air travel (where pressurized canisters
  are prohibited).
- **Limitations:** Non-adjustable flame; leaves a white residue on cookware; odor when burning.
- **Storage:** Keep dry; sealed in original packaging; tablets crumble if exposed to prolonged
  moisture.

## Safety Warnings

> ⚠️ **FIRE AND EXPLOSION HAZARD — Read all warnings before use.**

### General Safety (all models)

- **Never use a stove indoors, in a tent, or in any enclosed shelter.** All combustion stoves
  produce carbon monoxide (CO), an odorless, colorless, and lethal gas. Even small amounts in an
  enclosed space can cause unconsciousness and death. Use only in open air or a fully ventilated
  vestibule with the door open.
- **Keep stoves away from flammable materials.** Maintain a minimum 1-meter clearance from tent
  fabric, dry grass, leaves, and other combustibles.
- **Never leave a burning stove unattended.**
- **Allow stove to cool completely** before packing or handling.
- **Keep out of reach of children.**

### Canister Stove Safety (ACM-STOVE-001, ACM-STOVE-003)
- **Do not puncture, crush, incinerate, or expose canisters to heat above 50 °C.**
- **Check for leaks** before ignition: apply soapy water around the canister valve connection and
  look for bubbling. Do not ignite if a leak is detected.
- **Do not use damaged or dented canisters.** Even small dents can compromise pressure integrity.
- **Turn off the gas valve completely** when the stove is not in use.

### Liquid Fuel Stove Safety (ACM-STOVE-002)
- **Prime carefully.** Overfilling the priming cup causes a larger flare-up than intended. Use
  only a few drops of fuel for priming.
- **Do not overpressurize the fuel bottle.** Pump the bottle only as many strokes as directed
  (see pump body markings). Overpressurization can cause bottle failure.
- **Never fill the fuel bottle more than 80 %** — the pump requires an air column to function
  and overfilling eliminates the air space needed for pressurization.
- **Fill and light only with cold fuel** — do not fill a hot stove, and ensure the stove is
  cool before adding fuel.
- **Keep fuel away from open flame.** Spilled fuel ignites easily.

### Solid Fuel Safety (ACM-STOVE-004)
- **Hexamine tablets produce formaldehyde fumes.** Do not use in enclosed spaces; ventilate well.
- **Tablets are flammable** — store away from heat, flame, and sparks.
- **Do not eat or handle tablets without washing hands afterward.**

## Care & Maintenance

### After Each Use
- Let the stove cool fully before handling.
- For canister stoves: wipe the burner head with a dry cloth to remove food debris.
- For the AlpineFlame (ACM-STOVE-002): clean the jet using the included cleaning tool after
  every outing, or after every 2–3 uses when burning non-white-gas fuels.

### Seasonal Maintenance (ACM-STOVE-002)
- Rebuild the pump at least once per season using the ACM-STOVE-002-PUMP-KIT (includes
  replacement O-ring, cup washer, and lubricant).
- Inspect all fuel line connections for cracks and replace if found.
- Store fuel bottle empty, valve open, in a cool dry location.

### Storage
- Store all stoves clean, dry, and in their carrying cases.
- Remove canister from ACM-STOVE-001 and ACM-STOVE-003 before storing.
- Store solid fuel tablets sealed in their original packaging, away from moisture and heat.
