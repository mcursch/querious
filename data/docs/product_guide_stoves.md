# Product Guide: Stoves & Cooking Systems

## Overview

Acme Outfitters carries three categories of stoves: canister-gas, liquid fuel, and wood gasifier.
Each has distinct advantages depending on temperature, altitude, trip length, and personal preference.

## Canister Sizing Guide

| Canister Size | Good For |
|---|---|
| 100g | 1 person, 1–2 nights (boil water only) |
| 110g | 1 person, 2–3 nights |
| 227g | 1–2 people, 3–5 nights |
| 450g | 2–4 people, multi-day trips |

Rule of thumb: plan for 15–20g of fuel per person per day for simple meals (boil water, one pot).
For simmering, full cooking, or cold weather, increase to 25–30g per person per day.

## Altitude Performance

All isobutane stoves lose efficiency at altitude:
- Above 8,000 ft: boil times increase ~10%
- Above 12,000 ft: boil times increase ~25%
- Above 18,000 ft: boil times increase ~50%

Liquid fuel stoves maintain performance at altitude because fuel is vaporized by heat, not
pressure differential.

## Safety Notes

### General

- **Never use any stove inside a tent or enclosed shelter.** Carbon monoxide poisoning risk.
- Keep stoves and fuel away from open flames and heat sources.
- Allow the stove and fuel canister to cool completely before packing.

### Canister Stoves

- Do not puncture, incinerate, or over-pressurize fuel canisters.
- Do not use a canister that is dented, corroded, or has a damaged valve.
- In winter, warm the canister in your hands or sleeping bag before use to improve vaporization —
  never use an open flame to warm a canister.
- Store canisters in a cool location; do not leave in a hot vehicle.

### Liquid Fuel Stoves

- Priming involves burning a small amount of fuel — perform outdoors, away from dry vegetation.
- If the stove flares up during priming, stand back and let it burn down; do not blow it out.
- Inspect the fuel line and O-rings annually.
- Carry a stove maintenance kit on multi-week expeditions.

### Transporting Fuel

- Isobutane canisters are prohibited on commercial aircraft as carry-on or checked baggage.
- White gas must be fully drained before flying.
- Plan fuel resupply for flights or ship fuel in advance.

## Care & Maintenance

### Canister Stoves

- Clean burner with a soft brush to remove debris and pot residue.
- Check for bent or corroded fuel jet — replace if damaged.
- Store dry.

### Liquid Fuel Stoves

- After each season: disassemble pump, clean O-rings, re-lubricate with O-ring oil.
- Clean fuel line with the included cleaning cable.
- Test-burn white gas before trips to ensure complete combustion (blue flame, no yellow).
- Rebuild kits available: ACM-STOV-RBKIT ($12.99)
