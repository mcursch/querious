# Product Guide — Backpacks and Packs

*Acme Outfitters Pack Line, 2025 Edition*

## Overview

Acme Outfitters carries four pack families: **Summit Series** for technical alpine climbing, **Trail Series** for multi-day backpacking, **Dayhike Series** for one-day outings, and **Expedition Series** for extended expeditions and international travel. This guide covers volume guidance, fit systems, load-carrying specifications, and care instructions.

---

## Summit Series — Technical Alpine

Summit packs are designed for alpine starts, winter mountaineering, and ski tours where clean lines, crampon attachments, and ice-axe loops matter more than maximum comfort at heavy loads. Suspension is minimalist to keep weight and bulk out of the way during technical terrain.

### Summit 30

- **Volume:** 30 L
- **Trail weight:** 2 lb 6 oz (1,077 g)
- **Frame:** Internal aluminum framesheet, removable
- **Hip belt:** Lightly padded; removable for use as a climbing pack
- **Shoulder harness:** 3D foam contoured
- **Fabric:** 100D Dyneema Hybrid with 210D reinforcement base
- **Key features:** Top-access lid, crampon carry straps (external), ice-axe loop × 2, helmet-carry bungee, hydration sleeve (3 L compatible), side compression
- **Torso fit range:** 16–21 in (fixed)
- **Ideal load:** 20–35 lbs
- **Best for:** Single-day alpine climbs, ski tours, scrambles

### Summit 45

- **Volume:** 45 L
- **Trail weight:** 3 lb 4 oz
- **Frame:** Dual aluminum stays + framesheet
- **Hip belt:** Harness-style with gear loops; removable
- **Shoulder harness:** Dual-density foam, J-curve
- **Fabric:** Same as Summit 30
- **Key features:** All Summit 30 features plus sleeping-bag compartment, dual ice-axe loops, removable top lid converts to summit pack (8 L), U-zip front panel
- **Torso fit range:** 16–22 in (adjustable in 1-in increments)

- **Best for:** Multi-day alpine routes, expedition approach, winter hut trips

## Fit Guide

Proper fit is the most important factor in comfortable load-carrying. Follow these steps before purchasing or finalizing a fitting in-store.

### Step 1 — Measure Torso Length

Torso length is measured from the bony prominence at the top of your spine (C7 vertebra, felt at the base of your neck) to the top of your iliac crest (the bony ridge at the top of your hips). Have a second person measure for accuracy. Most adults fall between 14 and 22 inches.

### Step 2 — Measure Hip Circumference

Measure around your hips at the level of the iliac crest, not the waist. This determines the hipbelt size (S/M/L/XL). The hipbelt should wrap over your iliac crest; about 70–80% of the pack's weight should transfer to your hips.

### Step 3 — Adjust the Pack

1. Loosen all straps before loading.
2. Load the pack to at least 15 lbs for fitting.
3. Put the pack on and tighten the hipbelt first—centered on your iliac crest.
4. Tighten shoulder straps until they contour over your shoulders with no gap.
5. Clip and tighten the sternum strap (it should sit 1–2 in below your collarbone).
6. Pull the load-lifter straps (if present) until they make a 45° angle from your shoulder to the pack top.

### Volume Guidance

| Trip Length | Recommended Volume |
|-------------|-------------------|
| Half-day to full-day | 12–22 L |
| 1–2 nights | 30–45 L |
| 3–5 nights | 45–60 L |
| 6+ nights / heavy gear | 60–85 L |

These are guidelines only. An experienced ultralight traveler may cover a week with a 40 L pack; a beginner may need 65 L for a weekend.

## Care and Repair

### Cleaning

Hand-wash with warm water and a mild soap (not detergent) using a soft brush. Pay special attention to the hipbelt and shoulder harness, which accumulate sweat salt and skin oils that degrade foam over time. Rinse thoroughly. Air-dry completely in a shaded location before storage.

**Do not machine wash, tumble dry, or dry-clean backpacks.** Machine washing stresses seams and frame channels and can warp framesheet materials.

### Storage

Store packs loosely, not compressed, in a dry location away from UV. If the pack gets dirty inside, wipe out with a damp cloth. Leave zipper pulls unzipped slightly to reduce stress on zipper coils during storage.

### Repairs

Minor tears in pack fabric can be repaired with Seam Grip or Tenacious Tape at home. For structural repairs (broken frame stays, delaminated fabric, broken buckles), contact warranty@acmeoutfitters.example. Broken buckles are covered under warranty for the first year; beyond that, replacement buckles are available for purchase in our accessories section.
