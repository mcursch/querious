## Fit & Volume Guidance

Choosing the right pack involves two independent decisions: **torso fit** and **volume**. Getting
both right is critical for comfort, especially on multi-day trips.

### How to Measure Torso Length

Torso length (not height) determines which pack suspension will seat correctly on your body.

1. Tilt your head slightly forward. Feel for the bony C7 vertebra at the base of your neck — this
   is the top of your torso measurement.
2. Place your hands on your hips, thumbs pointing toward your back. The line across the top of your
   iliac crest (hip bones) is the bottom of your torso measurement.
3. Measure the straight-line distance along your spine between those two points.

| Torso length | Recommended packs |
|---|---|
| < 40 cm (< 15.5 in) | ACM-PACK-002 (TrailRunner 20L) |
| 40–44 cm (15.5–17.5 in) | ACM-PACK-001 (S yoke), ACM-PACK-003 (size S), ACM-PACK-004 |
| 44–50 cm (17.5–19.5 in) | ACM-PACK-001 (M yoke), ACM-PACK-002, ACM-PACK-003 (size M), ACM-PACK-004 |
| 50–54 cm (19.5–21 in) | ACM-PACK-001 (L yoke), ACM-PACK-003 (size L), ACM-PACK-004 |
| > 54 cm (> 21 in) | ACM-PACK-003 (size L; contact customer support for extended-torso options) |

### Hip Belt Sizing

Correct hipbelt fit transfers 60–80 % of pack weight onto your hips (not shoulders).

- Measure around your hips at the top of the iliac crest (same reference as torso measurement step 2).
- The hipbelt padding should wrap around so the buckle closes at the center of your abdomen with
  1–2 cm of adjustment room on each side.

| Hip circumference | Hip belt size (ACM-PACK-001 / -003) |
|---|---|
| 66–84 cm (26–33 in) | S/M |
| 84–102 cm (33–40 in) | M/L |
| 102–120 cm (40–47 in) | L/XL |

### Volume Recommendations by Trip Length

| Trip type | Recommended volume | Typical pack |
|---|---|---|
| Day hike (< 8 h, no overnight gear) | 10–25 L | ACM-PACK-002 or ACM-PACK-004 |
| Overnighter (1 night, warm season) | 25–40 L | ACM-PACK-004 or ACM-PACK-001 |
| Weekend trip (2–3 nights) | 40–55 L | ACM-PACK-001 |
| Extended backcountry (4–9 nights) | 55–75 L | ACM-PACK-003 |
| Expedition / winter (10+ nights) | 65–90 L | ACM-PACK-003 (supplement with lash-on exterior gear) |

> **Note:** These are guidelines. Heavier cold-weather or technical gear (ice axes, crampons,
> rope) increases required volume by ~15–20 L per category. Ultralight packers can often go one
> category lower.

## Loading & Adjustment Guidelines

1. **Bottom zone:** Sleeping bag and pad (lightest, bulkiest items).
2. **Core zone (closest to back):** Heavy items — food bear canister, water, fuel — to keep weight
   centered and close to your center of gravity.
3. **Top zone:** Frequently accessed items (rain jacket, snacks, first aid, navigation).
4. **Lid / external pockets:** Small quick-grab items only; avoid heavy objects high and far from
   your back.

After loading, follow this adjustment order for proper fit:
1. Hipbelt — fasten and tighten until padding sits on iliac crest.
2. Shoulder straps — pull snug with no daylight between straps and shoulders.
3. Load lifters — tighten to create a 45° angle between lifter strap and shoulder strap; do not
   over-tighten or the pack will pull away from your back.
4. Sternum strap — clip and adjust to a comfortable position across your chest; leave it slightly
   loose so your shoulders can move freely.

## Care & Maintenance

### Cleaning
- Empty all pockets and open all zippers before washing.
- Spot-clean with a damp sponge and mild non-detergent soap. For full cleaning, hand-wash in a
  bathtub with lukewarm water.
- Do **not** machine-wash — agitation can damage the frame, suspension foam, and coatings.
- Do **not** use bleach, dry-cleaning solvents, or fabric softeners.

### Drying
- Hang upside down in a shaded, well-ventilated area to drain water from the frame channel.
- Never dry in a clothes dryer or place near a direct heat source.

### DWR Maintenance
- The pack body has a DWR finish. Reapply with Nikwax Tent & Gear SolarProof or equivalent when
  water no longer beads on the surface.

### Zipper & Hardware
- Lubricate zipper teeth with wax-based lubricant once per season.
- Inspect buckles and straps for cracking. Replacement hardware is available from customer support.

### Storage
- Store uncompressed, in a breathable bag, away from UV light and heat sources.
- Remove the framesheet for long-term storage to avoid permanent bending.
