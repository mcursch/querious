## Pack Fitting Guide

A properly fitted pack transfers 70–80% of the load to your hips. Follow these steps:

### Step 1: Measure Torso Length

Torso length is **not** your height. Measure from the C7 vertebra (the bony bump at the base
of your neck) to the top of your hip bones (iliac crest).

| Torso Length | Size |
|---|---|
| Up to 15.5" | XS |
| 15.5"–17" | Small |
| 17"–18.5" | Medium |
| 18.5"–20" | Large |
| 20"+ | XL |

### Step 2: Load the Pack

Put 20–25 lbs in the pack (close to your expected trip load) before fitting.

### Step 3: Adjust the Hip Belt

- Loosen all straps before putting on the pack.
- Put on the pack and position the hip belt so the top edge sits **1 inch above your hip bones**.
- Buckle and tighten the hip belt snugly — you should not be able to fit more than 2 fingers.

### Step 4: Adjust Shoulder Straps

- Tighten shoulder straps so they curve around your shoulders without gaps.
- The load lifter straps (connecting the top of the shoulder straps to the pack) should form a
  30–45° angle. If they are slack, raise the shoulder harness or increase torso length.

### Step 5: Tighten the Sternum Strap

- Position at mid-chest and fasten snugly — tight enough to stabilize straps, not constrict breathing.

### Step 6: Walk & Adjust

Walk around for a few minutes and make final micro-adjustments. The pack should feel stable
without swaying.

## Volume Selection Guide

| Trip Type | Suggested Volume |
|---|---|
| Half-day to day hike | 10–20L |
| Overnight (ultralight) | 25–35L |
| 2–4 nights | 35–50L |
| 5–7 nights | 50–65L |
| 7+ nights or mountaineering | 65L+ |

Note: ultralight packers may choose one size smaller; heavy sleepers/campers may choose one
size larger.

## Care & Maintenance

### Cleaning

- Hand-wash with mild soap and lukewarm water. A soft brush works well on fabric.
- Do not machine-wash — this degrades the frame, coatings, and buckles.
- Do not dry-clean or use bleach.
- Air-dry completely before storage. Store upright in a cool, dry location.

### Storage

- Loosen all straps before long-term storage to prevent elastic fatigue.
- Do not store compressed — extended compression weakens foam padding.
- Inspect zippers, buckles, and stitching annually. Apply zipper lubricant (e.g., McNett Zip
  Care) if zippers feel stiff.

### Repairs

- Torn fabric, broken buckles, and broken zippers are all covered by our Lifetime Repair
  Program ($35 + shipping). See `warranty_policy.md` for details.
- Field repairs: carry a stick-on pack repair tape (available in our accessories) for emergency fixes.
