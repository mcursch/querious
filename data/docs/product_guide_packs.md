## Pack Series

### Summit Series — Ultralight & Fast-and-Light

Summit packs are minimalist, frameless or semi-frame packs intended for day hikes, summit pushes, and fast-and-light alpine routes where weight is the top priority and loads stay under 25 lbs.

**Volume range:** 18L – 36L

**Key features:**
- Frameless or stay-frame construction (removable aluminum stay in 28L+ models)
- Stretchy front mesh pocket for trail snacks, maps, and wet layers
- Hydration-compatible (reservoir sleeve + left/right port routing)
- Hipbelt: thin, padded wrap-around — functional for stabilization, not load transfer above 20 lbs
- Shoulder-strap adjustment: load lifters present on 28L and 36L models

**Fit:** Summit packs are sized S/M and M/L based on torso length. Measure torso length (C7 vertebra to iliac crest) to select size. Most adults 5'2″–5'8″ fit S/M; 5'8″+ fit M/L.

**Best for:** Day hikes, fast-and-light alpine, ski touring approaches, trail running with gear

### Traverse Series — Multi-Day Backpacking

The Traverse series is our core backpacking line, covering 3–7 day trips with loads of 25–50 lbs. These packs feature a full suspension system with an adjustable torso length, a load-transfer hipbelt, and structured framesheet.

**Volume range:** 45L – 70L

**Key features:**
- Adjustable torso length (4 cm range per size): fit can be dialed after purchase
- Load-transfer hipbelt with padded, contoured wings — transfers up to 80% of pack weight to hips
- Full-height aluminum frame stays (removable and bendable for custom fit)
- Top lid converts to a summit pack (detaches and adds a shoulder strap)
- Front shove-it pocket, hip belt pockets (2), side water-bottle pockets (2), sleeping-bag compartment with divider
- Rain cover included on all Traverse models

**Fit guide:**

| Torso Length | Pack Size | Fits Heights (approximate) |
|---|---|---|
| Under 16″ | XS | 5'0″ and under |
| 16″–18″ | S | 5'0″ – 5'5″ |
| 18″–20″ | M | 5'4″ – 5'10″ |
| Over 20″ | L | 5'9″ and over |

Note: torso length, not overall height, determines pack fit. Always measure torso length rather than estimating from height alone.

**Best for:** Weekend to 7-day backpacking, hiking, international trekking

### Expedition Series — Heavy Load & Extended Trips

The Expedition series is built for loads over 50 lbs and trips lasting more than a week — bushwhacking, mountaineering approaches, portage trips, and long-distance thru-hikes with full food carries.

**Volume range:** 65L – 95L

**Key features:**
- Dual aluminum frame stays + peripheral framesheet for maximum structure under load
- Hipbelt with integrated lumbar pad — padded lower back contact reduces fatigue on heavy loads
- Compression straps (4 total) to tighten the load when not fully packed
- Front U-zip access to main compartment (no need to unpack from the top to reach mid-bag items)
- Detachable top lid with two compartments
- Trekking pole attachment loops on front panel
- Durable Cordura® 500D base fabric with 210D ripstop upper panels

**Fit:** Same torso-length sizing as Traverse series. Hipbelt is sold separately in 4 sizes (XS–XL) for a precise hip fit, especially important at high loads.

**Best for:** Mountaineering, 10+ day backpacking, portage canoe tripping, expedition resupply

### Daypack Series — Urban & Light Trail Use

Lightweight lifestyle and trail daypacks for commuters, urban users, gym-goers, and single-day hikers.

**Volume range:** 16L – 28L

**Key features:**
- Laptop sleeve (fits up to 15″ laptops in 20L+ models)
- Padded back panel for comfort and air flow
- Simple top-load or panel-load designs
- Available in a wider range of color options than technical packs

**Best for:** Commuting, school, day hikes under 5 miles, travel

## Fitting Your Pack

Proper fit is the single most important factor in comfort and injury prevention on long trips. Follow these steps at home before your first trip:

1. **Measure your torso length.** Have a partner locate your C7 vertebra (the prominent bump at the base of your neck when you tilt your head forward) and your iliac crest (the top of your hip bones). Measure the distance along the curve of your spine. This is your torso length.
2. **Set the torso length** on adjustable packs (Traverse and Expedition). Loosen the shoulder-strap attachment, set to the appropriate mark, and re-secure.
3. **Load the pack** with about 20 lbs for a test fit. Empty packs cannot be properly fitted.
4. **Fasten the hipbelt** first. The hipbelt padding should sit centered on your iliac crest — about 1″ above your hip bones. Tighten until snug but not painful.
5. **Pull the shoulder straps** down and back until the contoured back fits flush against your back. The shoulder-strap anchor points should sit 1–2 inches below the top of your shoulders.
6. **Tighten the load lifters** (the short straps at the top of the shoulder straps running to the top of the pack). They should form a 45° angle to horizontal. Over-tight load lifters indicate the torso length is too long.
7. **Tighten the sternum strap** across your chest for lateral stability, keeping it loose enough to breathe comfortably.

## Load Management Tips

- Keep heavy items (food, water, shelter) close to your back and near your center of gravity.
- Place frequently accessed items (snacks, rain gear, headlamp) in top-lid pockets and hip-belt pockets.
- Distribute weight evenly left-to-right to avoid lateral imbalance on uneven terrain.
- For scrambling or technical terrain, cinch all compression straps tightly and stow dangling straps to prevent snagging.

## Care Instructions

- **Hand-wash or machine-wash on gentle cycle** in cold water with mild soap. Use a mesh laundry bag.
- **Do not machine-dry or tumble dry.** Hang to air-dry out of direct sunlight.
- **Do not use bleach, solvents, or fabric softeners.** These degrade the DWR coating and fabric integrity.
- **Inspect zippers, buckles, and stitching** after each multi-day trip and treat zippers with a silicone-based lubricant if they feel stiff.
- **Store loosely** in a cool, dry place. Do not store compressed inside a stuff sack long-term.
