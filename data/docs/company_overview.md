# Acme Outfitters – Company Overview

*Last updated: January 2024*

---

## 1. Our Story

Acme Outfitters was founded in **1987** by avid mountaineer and trail runner Clara Beckett in Boulder, Colorado. What began as a single-room shop selling hand-stitched packs and locally sourced trail maps has grown into one of the most respected specialty outdoor-gear retailers in North America. Clara's guiding philosophy — that the right gear should be accessible to everyone who wants to explore the outdoors, from weekend hikers to serious alpinists — still shapes every decision the company makes today.

Over nearly four decades, Acme Outfitters has expanded its product range to cover hiking, climbing, camping, paddling, cycling, and winter sports. The company sources from more than 200 brands worldwide while also producing its own house-label gear under the **Summit Select** private label. As of 2024, Acme Outfitters operates fourteen retail stores, a robust e-commerce platform, and a B2B wholesale division that supplies outfitting programs at national parks and outdoor education organizations.

## 2. Mission Statement

> **"We equip people to go further — in the backcountry, in their communities, and in themselves."**

Acme Outfitters is committed to connecting customers with the knowledge and equipment they need to pursue adventure responsibly. We believe that time spent outdoors builds healthier people and stronger communities, and we measure our success not only in revenue but in the number of first-time hikers we help get on trail, the environmental initiatives we fund through our **1% for Trails** pledge, and the culture of belonging we cultivate in every store.

Acme Outfitters operates under a functional organizational model with five top-level departments, each led by a C-suite or VP-level executive who reports directly to the Chief Executive Officer (CEO).

### 4.2 Retail & Store Operations

This department is responsible for the day-to-day management of all fourteen physical retail locations, including staffing, visual merchandising, inventory replenishment, and in-store customer experience. Regional managers for the West, Central, and East zones report to the VP of Retail Operations, who in turn reports to the COO.

### 4.3 E-Commerce & Technology

The Technology department owns the company's digital channels, including the e-commerce platform, mobile app, internal business systems, data infrastructure, and the IT security function. It is divided into four sub-teams: Platform Engineering, Data & Analytics, IT Operations, and Cybersecurity. The CIO leads this department.

### 4.4 Merchandising & Buying

This department selects and purchases the products Acme Outfitters carries across all channels. Category buyers are organized by discipline: Hiking & Trail Running, Climbing & Mountaineering, Camp & Travel, Paddle Sports, Cycling, and Winter Sports. The VP of Merchandising coordinates closely with the Summit Select private-label team to fill assortment gaps.

### 4.5 Marketing & Brand

Marketing manages brand identity, advertising, content, public relations, social media, email marketing, loyalty programs, and community partnerships. The **Acme Ambassador Program** — a network of sponsored athletes and outdoor educators — falls under this department. The CMO leads this function.

### 4.6 Finance & Accounting

The Finance department covers financial planning and analysis (FP&A), accounting, accounts payable, accounts receivable, treasury, and tax compliance. Finance also owns the vendor payment processes and the corporate travel policy. The CFO leads this department and provides reporting to the company's board of directors.

### 4.7 People & Culture (HR)

People & Culture covers talent acquisition, onboarding, total rewards (compensation and benefits), learning and development, employee relations, and diversity, equity, and inclusion (DEI) initiatives. The Chief People Officer leads this department and partners closely with all other functional heads on workforce planning.

## 5. Values and Culture

Acme Outfitters operates according to five core values that guide hiring decisions, product choices, and business practices:

1. **Adventure First** — We are outdoors people ourselves, and we bring that passion to everything we do.
2. **Radical Inclusion** — The outdoors belongs to everyone. We actively work to reduce barriers — cost, knowledge, and access — for underrepresented communities.
3. **Environmental Stewardship** — We minimize our footprint, fund trail maintenance through our 1% for Trails pledge, and prioritize suppliers with strong sustainability practices.
4. **Expertise You Can Trust** — Our staff are trained specialists. We invest in education so that every customer gets genuine, experience-backed advice.
5. **Long-Term Thinking** — We make decisions for the next decade, not the next quarter.
