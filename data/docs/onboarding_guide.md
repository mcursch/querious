# New Employee Onboarding Guide — Acme Outfitters

*For: All new full-time and part-time employees*
*Maintained by: HR & Engineering*
*Questions: hr@acmeoutfitters.example or your assigned onboarding buddy*

---

## Welcome

Congratulations and welcome to Acme Outfitters! This guide walks you through everything you need to do in your first 30 days to get set up, oriented, and productive. Your hiring manager and assigned onboarding buddy will help you navigate anything not covered here.

Your first week will feel busy — that is normal. The goal is not to know everything by day 5; it is to meet your teammates, understand your role, and have the systems access you need to do your job. Everything else follows.

---

## Before Day 1 — Pre-Hire Checklist (HR Completes)

HR will send you a pre-hire packet before your start date. You should receive:

- [ ] Offer letter and signed employment agreement
- [ ] Benefits enrollment forms (deadline: within 30 days of start date)
- [ ] Direct deposit setup link (via Paylocity)
- [ ] IT equipment shipping confirmation (for remote employees) or pick-up instructions (for on-site)
- [ ] Background check completion confirmation

If any of the above are missing by 2 business days before your start date, email hr@acmeoutfitters.example immediately.

## Week 1 — Orientation & Access

### Day 1

| Time | Activity |
|---|---|
| 9:00 AM | Welcome call with HR (remote) / in-person HR orientation (on-site) |
| 10:00 AM | Meet your manager and team |
| 11:00 AM | IT setup — laptop, accounts, VPN |
| 1:00 PM | Lunch with your onboarding buddy |
| 2:00 PM | Company overview session (recorded if you're remote) |
| 3:30 PM | Department intro and Q&A with your manager |

### Systems Access Checklist

Your IT contact (it@acmeoutfitters.example) will provision the following systems. Confirm you have access to each by end of Day 2:

- [ ] **Google Workspace** (acmeoutfitters.example email, Drive, Calendar, Meet) — should be ready Day 1 morning
- [ ] **Slack** — invitation sent to personal email Day 1; migrate to work email after login
- [ ] **Jira** — project and task tracking (all departments)
- [ ] **Confluence** — internal wiki and documentation
- [ ] **Paylocity** — payroll and HR self-service (submit direct deposit, view pay stubs, request PTO)
- [ ] **Salesforce** — Sales and Support staff only; request from your manager if needed
- [ ] **GitHub** — Engineering staff only; submit your GitHub username to IT
- [ ] **Warehouse Management System (WMS)** — Warehouse staff only; on-site training on Day 2

**Password policy:** All accounts must use a strong password (minimum 14 characters, mixed case, numbers, and symbols). You must enable multi-factor authentication (MFA) on your Google Workspace account and Salesforce account before end of Day 2. See the Security Policy for full password requirements.

### Mandatory Training

The following training modules must be completed in your first week via the Confluence learning portal:

1. **Code of Conduct & Ethics** (~30 min)
2. **Data Security & Privacy Basics** (~45 min)
3. **Workplace Safety** (~20 min)
4. **Harassment Prevention** (~45 min) — California and New York employees have a 2-hour extended version

Completion is tracked automatically. Your manager will receive a notification when all four modules are complete.

## Weeks 2–4 — Role Ramp-Up

### 30-Day Onboarding Meetings

Schedule the following meetings in your first 30 days (your buddy can help arrange):

- [ ] 1:1 with each member of your immediate team
- [ ] 1:1 with a representative from each department you'll work closely with
- [ ] "How we work" session with your manager (covering sprint/project cadence, communication norms, and priorities)
- [ ] Product walkthrough with the Product or Sales team (highly recommended for all staff — understanding what we sell is foundational)

### Role-Specific Ramp Plans

Your manager will provide a written 30-60-90 day ramp plan during your first week. The ramp plan defines:
- Key deliverables for each 30-day period
- People to meet and knowledge areas to master
- The "onboarding complete" criteria used in your 90-day review

### 90-Day Review

At the end of your 90-day onboarding period, you and your manager will complete a brief written review assessing your ramp progress. This review is used to:
- Confirm your employment status (at-will; 90-day review is not a probationary period in the legal sense)
- Determine eligibility for hybrid remote work (available after 90 days with a satisfactory review — see the Employee Handbook)
- Identify any training or support needs going forward
